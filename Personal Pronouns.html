German Personal Pronouns

In German grammar, Personalpronomen (personal pronouns) change depending on the case used in the sentence.

The three most common cases are:

  • Nominativ – the person doing the action
  • Akkusativ – the person receiving the action directly
  • Dativ – the person to whom or for whom something is done

Nominativ (Subject)

Ich lerne Deutsch.
(I learn German.)
Er spielt Fußball.
(He plays football.)

Akkusativ (Direct Object)

Er sieht mich.
(He sees me.)
Ich liebe dich.
(I love you.)

Dativ (Indirect Object)

Er gibt mir ein Buch.
(He gives me a book.)
Ich helfe dir.
(I help you.)
Quick Tip:
Nominativ → Who does the action?
Akkusativ → What or whom is affected?
Dativ → To whom or for whom?
